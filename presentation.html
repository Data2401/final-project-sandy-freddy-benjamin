Key Findings

Anxiety: Shows an upward trend in “Extreme” gaming levels.

Social Context: Playing with strangers correlates with higher Social Anxiety compared to playing with friends.

Motivation: Players gaming for “Relaxation” and “Fun” report higher Life Satisfaction than those gaming for “Competition.”

Final Thoughts: Gaming’s effect on well-being depends heavily on how and why you play.

What does it mean?

Well-being remains stable through moderate gaming, but we see a tipping point in the Extreme category (>30 hours/week) where anxiety begins to rise.

Social Anxiety isn’t tied to how much you play, but who you play with. Playing with Friends results in lower social anxiety than playing with strangers or alone.

Gamers motivated by fun and relaxation score significantly higher in life satisfaction than those driven primarily by competition or escapism.

Gaming doesn’t affect everyone the same way. Our data suggests the well-being of a gamer is determined by the balance between their gaming hours and their social context.

What We Suggest

If you’re playing games, you should pay attention to:

How much you play

Who you’re playing with

Why you’re playing

If all three of these reasons align with actions that don’t bring you stress then your overall well-being will increase rather than decrease.
